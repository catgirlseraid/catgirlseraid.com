About Me

I started editing in June 2024 after learning about OSM from a friend. They saw me editing for closed sourced maps and recommended I try contributing to OSM, as users have greater control over their edits, plus all the other benefits that come with open-source projects. In the beginning, I focused on smaller tasks like updating road alignments, but now I work on almost every aspect of OSM. It's very rewarding to know that my work improves people's lives, whether it's roads in rural Ethiopia or footpaths and crossings in urban New Zealand.


I vary what I work on, from resolving notes where possible to micromapping universities. I particularly enjoy mapping buildings, solar panels and urban areas, working with landuse, data validation, fixing incorrect tags and geometry, but i work on almost every type of data in OSM. Below you'll find a list of some of the major projects I've completed or worked on, as well as areas I've micromapped. I also created and work on the Detailing of Solar Infrastructure (Worldwide) project

Detailing of Solar Infrastructure (Worldwide)

I'm currently working on the Detailing of Solar Infrastructure (Worldwide) project I created in May 2025. This involved writing the wiki page, writing associated overpass querys to find the data, doing trial mapping to see if the instructions made sense and worked in the real world as well as detailing the scope and all project instructions. This project is designed to add more data to the worlds solar farms. This is accomplished by mapping solar panels in higher detail, adding missing solar farms and adding more detailed tagging. This project converts solar panels to smaller, higher detail areas, and removes solar panel ways. Solar panel nodes may also be converted to areas as part of this project, although it is not the main goal. This is useful for both map rendering, and data consumers who need information about solar panels or solar farms.
